Version 15 - Simplified savings and investments

IN DESIGN

Iterate current S&I journeys

Our initial savings and investment investigation (V12) has highlighted that the information we require would be too difficult for the citizen to complete until we have a way they can return to the application. The complexity of data required would almost always lead to a session timeout requiring the citizen to start the whole application again.

Therefore we recommend we address the immediate painpoint with a iterated design. We look to improve"

  1. The upfront knowledge a citizen requires to complete the task
  2. The description of capital types from savings and investments to money you have
  3. Look to see if we can add further inprovements to the journey based on what we now know (nice to have at this stage).

Date of iteration: December 2024
Designers: Mark Keeling and Matt Hall

Version 14 - Catagorisation proof of concept

IN DESIGN

Catagorisation (cross cutting)

Work to identify potential payment cases to progress more quickly.

Assumed needs

  1. As an agent I need to quickly access all information a CAM record requires so a decision to be made
  2. As a team leader I need to understand the complexity and likely outcome of a claim so that I can allocate it to the right team to ensure payments are made quickly.
  3. As a member of the development team I need to know the actual outcome of a claim so I can improve the decision engine

Date of iteration: November 2024
Designers: Mark Keeling and Matt Hall

Version 7 - Redefinition of MVP

IN DESIGN

Changes to agent process to deliver a consistent task for both award types.

This continues the work of V6 but has been refactored based on the new view of MVP decided on Thursday 29th February

  1. Regrouping of task processes based on December 2023 UR
  2. Removal of DoD check as this can be automated
  3. Reduce the amount of agent screens by grouping checks to a single screen based on December 2023 UR
  4. Addition of Cold Weather Payment Task (pay only)
  5. Addition of task to check for other household members (pay only)
  6. A number of previously considered agent tasks are now automated using data we consume within the service. The include benefits (income), earnings and immigration status
  7. A further decision was taken to use the name from CIS/Searchlight directly within the letter to remove the reputational damage of incorrectly formatted names

Date of design: March 2024
Designers: Mark Keeling and Matt Hall
